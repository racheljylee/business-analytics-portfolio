# Business Performance Review — Data Visualization
`Power BI · Business Analytics · Data Storytelling`

A 5-year business performance review (2020–Q1 2025) for eVery, a health supplements e-commerce company. Built as part of the Master in Business Analytics & Data Science at IE University, this project translates raw sales data into an executive-facing Power BI report covering revenue trends, platform and category mix, and regional performance.

---

## Project Overview

The report surfaces three core business questions:

1. How has eVery's revenue and volume trended over 5 years, and what does Q1 2025 signal?
2. Which platforms and product categories are driving — or dragging — performance?
3. Where are the regional opportunities and risks, and what should the business do about them?

---

## Key Findings

**Overall Performance**
- Q1 2025 revenue declined 2.24% YoY, continuing a post-2022 softening trend after peak revenue of 1.22M in Q1 2022
- Annual average YoY revenue growth over the 5-year period remains marginally positive at +0.62%

**Platform & Category Mix**
- Platform revenue share is relatively stable across iHerb, Amazon, and Walmart — though Walmart shows a slight declining trend
- Vitamin and Mineral consistently account for ~33% of annual revenue, forming the core of the product portfolio

**Regional Analysis**
- Canada is the most consistent revenue driver, maintaining ~34–35% revenue share across years
- UK shows an overall downward demand trend, with early signs of recovery in Q1 2025 — identified as the top near-term focus area
- USA presents a growth opportunity, with flat volume but a clearer upward revenue trend

**UK Deep Dive**
- Mineral is UK's top-selling category by volume (18.8%) but lags in revenue (16.9%) — a 1.9pp gap signalling pricing pressure
- Discount analysis reveals a slight negative correlation between discount depth and units sold for Mineral, suggesting discounting is not driving incremental volume
- Fat Burner and Performance are identified as high-value, under-indexed categories worth prioritising for investment

---

## Recommendations

1. Re-evaluate pricing and discount strategies across categories and platforms, prioritising revenue conversion
2. Reduce discounts within the Mineral category, as discounting does not show positive top-line results
3. Prioritise investment in high-value categories — Fat Burner and Performance — to accelerate revenue recovery in UK
