# Hit Prediction & Marketing Investment Strategy (Music Industry)

## Overview

**Group Project**

Demonstrated that prioritizing hit discovery (vs minimizing wasted spend) can increase profits by ~45%.

This project develops a predictive framework to support **marketing investment decisions** in the music industry.

---

## My Contribution

- Structured the **business problem (investment vs risk trade-off)**  
- Led translation of model results into **financial and strategic insights**  
- Developed **economic simulation and business case**  
- Delivered recommendations focused on **capital allocation strategy**  

---

## Business Problem

Record labels face:

- High uncertainty in predicting hit songs  
- Inefficient allocation of marketing budgets  
- Missed breakout opportunities  

**Key question:**  
*How should labels allocate marketing spend to maximize returns?*

---

## Approach

### 1. Predictive Modeling
- Tested:
  - Decision Tree
  - Random Forest
  - **XGBoost (best performing model)**  

- Optimized for:
  - accuracy
  - precision
  - generalization  

---

### 2. Feature Analysis

Key finding:

> **Audio features matter more than genre in predicting success**

Top drivers:
- Loudness  
- Energy  
- Acousticness  

---

## Key Insights

- **Hit potential is predictable**
  - Certain audio characteristics consistently outperform  

- **The real trade-off is economic**
  - False positives → wasted budget  
  - False negatives → missed hits (higher cost)  

- **Missing hits is more expensive than wasting spend**

---

## Strategic Simulation

Two strategies compared:

- Conservative (limit wasted spend)  
- Opportunity-focused (capture more hits)  

**Result:**
- Opportunity-focused approach generated significantly higher profit (~€43.8M vs €30.0M)

---

## Strategic Recommendations

- **Shift toward hit discovery (maximize upside)**
- **Invest earlier in high-potential tracks**
- **Adopt tiered marketing allocation strategies**

---
